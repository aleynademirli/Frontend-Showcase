Jane Austen (1775–1817, England)

Jane Austen is an English writer known for her subtle humor and sharp observations of social relationships in 19th-century England.

Her novels mainly explore themes such as love, marriage, social class, and the role of women in society.

Important Works

Pride and Prejudice

A novel exploring love, marriage, and social class through sharp social observation and character development.

Sense and Sensibility

A story contrasting emotion and reason through the lives of two sisters facing love and loss.

Emma

A novel focusing on self-awareness, personal growth, and social responsibility.

“It is a truth universally acknowledged, that a single man in possession of a good fortune, must be in want of a wife.”

Halide Edip Adıvar (1884–1964, Turkey)

Halide Edip Adıvar is one of the first Turkish women writers and an influential figure in modern Turkish literature.

Her literary works often explore themes such as nationalism, women's roles in society, education, and the tension between tradition and modernity.

Important Works

Sinekli Bakkal

A novel depicting social transformation in Ottoman society through religion, tradition, and modernization.

Ateşten Gömlek

One of the first novels about the Turkish War of Independence, focusing on patriotism and sacrifice.

Handan

A psychological novel exploring a woman’s emotional conflicts and search for identity.

“There are moments in the life of nations when sacrifice becomes the greatest measure of all things.”
